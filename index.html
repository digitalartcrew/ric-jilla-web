The Story

Indiana's
Own.

Ric Jilla is a trailblazing MC from the state of Indiana — a region not typically associated with hip-hop royalty, but one that produced a true pioneer. With gritty lyricism, undeniable stage presence, and a relentless work ethic, Ric Jilla carved his name into the culture at a time when Indiana was largely invisible on the rap map.

As one of the first rappers from Indiana to be featured on BET, Ric Jilla opened doors and proved that the Midwest had something to say. His music reflects the resilience, hustle, and heart of Indiana — authentic stories delivered with the skill of a veteran MC.

Beyond the booth, Ric Jilla is committed to giving back through the Brother Jill Foundation — uplifting communities and creating opportunities for the next generation.

Giving Back

Brother Jill
Foundation.

The Brother Jill Foundation is Ric Jilla's commitment to something bigger than music. Born from a deep sense of responsibility to the community that shaped him, the foundation works to create real opportunities for youth and families across Indiana.

Through mentorship, outreach, and community-driven programs, the Brother Jill Foundation carries forward the belief that success is meaningless without giving back. Ric Jilla walks the walk — not just as an artist, but as a pillar of his community.

The foundation stands as proof that hip-hop is more than entertainment — it's a vehicle for change, for uplift, and for building something that lasts beyond the music.
